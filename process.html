route How I Work

A process built for change, not content delivery.

Most learning initiatives fail the same way — great content, no impact. My process stays anchored to the one thing that matters: where do we need to get to, and what needs to change to make this possible.

Principles

Three things I hold on to.

target

Performance is what matters

In any project, understand where you are and where you want to be. The bit in between is the performance gap. It is the focus of the work, understanding the cause and finding effective bridges to help people across.

insights

Let the data lead

Analytics, CSAT, support tickets, real floor observations — the evidence usually knows where the real problem sits. Opinions only come in after the data has spoken.

bolt_boost

Prototype early, iterate often

Showing my stakeholder a demo of an idea before full development or a learner a rough scenario teaches me more than a month of conversations without a tangible focus. I build small, test fast, and let the feedback rewrite the plan.

The Process

Six phases, from gap to evidence.

Rooted in ADDIE and SAM, sharpened with Action Mapping and GAGNE's 9 Events and the discipline of an Agile sprint — tuned to each project's shape.

01

Listen & Discover

What problem are we actually solving?

Before I write a line of content, I sit with the people closest to the problem — stakeholders, SMEs, and the learners themselves. I pull the hard numbers too: NPS, CSAT, compliance rates, support tickets. The goal is to separate the stated problem from the real one. Whilst many stakeholders come with this data, getting a performance and learning lens on it helps to identify otherwise unseen opportunities.

02

Frame the Change

What do we need people to do differently?

This is where Action Mapping lays the foundations. I translate the problem into the specific, observable skills, behaviours or knowledge that would fix it — then write learning objectives, audience personas and measurable outcomes against each one. It's where a course brief stops being a content list and starts being a plan for change. THIS learner, THIS problem and needs to do THIS to fix it.

03

Prototype & Storyboard

Does it land before we commit?

I'd rather fail in a basic wireframe than in a launched course. Simple storyboards, scenario scripts and a single playable slice go in front of SMEs and sample learners early. Cheap to change, brutal at exposing weak ideas, and excellent at rapid enhancement — exactly as it should be.

04

Build with Intent

Production with a point of view.

Build phase is where the tool choice finally matters. What were the requirements, were there any technical limitations, how does it need to be accessed, how rich does the interactivity need to be, and how quickly is it required. Articulate 360 for rich interactivity, Evolve for responsive scale, 7Taps for deskless microlearning, Copilot Studio when a chat-based agent is the right shape. AI speeds drafts, scripts and asset generation; my job is to keep it honest against the objectives.

05

Launch & Enable

Make the rollout part of the design.

Great content gets buried without a launch plan and the right wrap-around. I set up the LMS, write the comms, build the manager toolkits and make sure there's somewhere to turn when a learner is stuck on the floor at 2pm on a Tuesday. Enablement is design, not an afterthought.

06

Measure & Evolve

Prove it — or fix it.

Launch is the halfway point. I track the metrics agreed back in Phase 2, watch the behaviour data come in, and feed the findings into a v2. Some changes are tweaks, some rewrite the brief entirely. That's the job — learning the thing you built, alongside the people using it.

How I Collaborate

A steady hand through the messy middle.

I've delivered solo on tight rollouts and led distributed teams across remote, matrix and onsite setups. Whichever shape the project takes, I work in short, visible cycles — share work early, ask for feedback often, and keep a running record of what's been decided and why.

Stakeholders get the clarity they need, SMEs feel heard, and learners end up with something that respects their time.

  • Weekly or bi-weekly stakeholder check-ins
  • Shared roadmap & decision log
  • Clear working drafts, no surprise reveals
  • Post-launch review baked into the timeline
