Pillar 01 rocket_launch

Implementation & Strategy

Getting it right before go-live.

Learners make decisions on systems quickly. A good first impression counts, and the work to achieve this starts way ahead of any rollout. Scoping against capability, understanding business requirements, preparing the people, translating technical spec into plain language, and treating QA as rigorous validation — not a tickbox.

  • check_circle
    Needs Analysis & Scoping Aligning business requirements to platform capability before the build begins.
  • check_circle
    Change Management Preparing the business, administrators, managers and learners so adoption lands from day one.
  • check_circle
    Technical Translation Turning technical specs into language stakeholders actually understand.
  • check_circle
    Quality Assurance Rigorous validation that the system behaves exactly as intended before go-live.
Outcome: A system that actually fits the business and achieves their goals
Pillar 02 verified_user

Platform Ownership & Governance

The long-term product owner mindset.

A well-run LMS or LXP doesn't happen by accident, with poorly defined governance and processes creating a conflicted mess quickly. Clean taxonomy, processes defined, data that gets interpreted, updates tested properly, and relationships that track evolving goals.

  • check_circle
    Strategic Governance Standardised processes and taxonomy rules that keep the platform clean and searchable.
  • check_circle
    Data-Driven Optimisation Reading learner data to continuously tweak and improve the platform.
  • check_circle
    Release Management Testing vendor updates in a staging environment before rolling them out.
  • check_circle
    Stakeholder Alignment Managing ongoing business relationships as company goals evolve.
  • check_circle
    Content Management Ensuring that content is clearly defined and well structured.
Outcome: A platform that stays relevant as the business evolves
Pillar 03 favorite

Learner-Centric Experience Design

How it feels, not just how it functions.

This is where an LXD background shows up in systems work. WCAG-aligned hubs, strong mobile uptake, frictionless HR-stack connections, and user-listening loops that drive UI iteration — not launch-and-forget.

  • check_circle
    Accessible and Multi-modal Designing a platform that meets WCAG standards and works just as well in a learner's hands as at their desk, through increased app uptake.
  • check_circle
    Hyper-personalisation Learners don't want to hunt for development. They've been taught that algorithms will provide what they need. Making an LMS/LXP meet this expectation turns it into a destination of choice.
  • check_circle
    Frictionless Integrations Experience mapping that connects the platform smoothly with the wider HR stack, external content libraries and core systems where users are.
  • check_circle
    Curated Journeys Moving from overwhelming catalogues to targeted, branded, relevant pathways. Make discovery simpler and easier.
  • check_circle
    Continuous Listening Learner feedback loops set up to guide the needs and evolution of the system to ensure it continues to serve.
Outcome: Learners who use it because they want to
